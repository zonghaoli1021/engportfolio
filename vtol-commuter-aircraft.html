01 // DESIGN BRIEF

Connecting vertical flight with practical urban parking.

The Challenge

Conventional rotorcraft do not fit comfortably within typical parking-garage clearances. The team investigated a compact vehicle that could land vertically on a rooftop, reduce its active rotor envelope, and enter the garage without relying on a runway.

The Concept

The proposed design uses a two-blade single main rotor, a Lycoming O-360 engine, wheeled skids, a stowable main rotor, and a tail rotor that swivels to provide thrust during low-speed ground travel.

02 // CONCEPT OF OPERATIONS

One vehicle, two operating environments.

01

Depart

Vertical takeoff from a residential origin without a large runway.

02

Cruise

Air transit toward an urban destination with a 100 nm design-range objective.

03

Land

Vertical arrival on the rooftop of a city parking structure.

04

Reconfigure

Stow the main rotor and swivel the tail rotor for controlled ground movement.

05

Park

Drive through garage clearances at a limited ground speed of about 12 mph.

03 // CAD DEVELOPMENT

Packaging the flight system for a constrained footprint.

04 // TRADEOFFS & VALIDATION

A promising concept with clearly documented next steps.

DESIGN RESPONSE

Compact operating envelope

The design investigation addressed garage compatibility through rotor stowage, a swiveling tail rotor, and low-speed ground operation.

KEY TRADEOFF

Weight objective exceeded

The final presentation reports a 1,096 lb design weight against the original objective of less than 1,000 lb, identifying weight reduction as future optimization work.

REQUIRED VALIDATION

Before physical development

The report identifies structural testing, rotor-dynamic analysis, failure-mode analysis, professional review, and aviation regulatory compliance as required next steps.
